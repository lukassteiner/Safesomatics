What is somatic therapy?

Fluid, non-linear process

Works directly with your nervous system and the innate intelligence of your body.

It's not talk therapy

There's no need to dissect your past or build explanations for who you are.

Body oriented

We work with what your body is expressing in the present moment.

It's about tuning into what's happening right now, in this moment, in your body. It works with our oldest part of our nervous system which handles the automatic functions in our bodies, namely the Autonomic Nervous System: responsible for survival, rest & digest and for our capacity for social engagement.

There is nothing wrong with you

Unhealed trauma is stored in our bodies.

We experience this somatically in the form of sensations, behaviors, emotions, feelings, thoughts and sometimes even chronic pain and disease.

Entire identities, beliefs and cultural biases are created out of traumatic responses. Part of our brain gets activated under real or perceived danger and gets stuck as a way of protecting us and ensure our survival. Isn't that amazing?

Autonomic Nervous System

Our Autonomic Nervous System is this incredible protective part within us that supports us being safe, always scanning the environment for danger cues. This is felt through either the Sympathetic Nervous System or our Dorsal Vagal Complex (Parasympathetic Nervous System). Meaning, in our search for safety, we can get stuck either in fight/flight or in freeze/collapse for long periods of time, sometimes even since childhood.

A dysregulated nervous system is incredibly common in our western society and with mainstream lifestyle encouraging efficiency and isolation.

Our nervous system is still wired to live within a nurturing community or tribe. Yet, it has become the norm for us to cherish independence in isolated cubicles, in overcrowded concrete jungles.

Traumatic stress stuck in our bodies is the main cause of unhappiness and struggles in our relationships with our loved ones or in our professional life.

Traumatic symptoms are not caused by the event itself. They arise when residual energy from the experience is not discharged from the body. This energy remains trapped in the nervous system where it can wreak havoc on our bodies and minds.
